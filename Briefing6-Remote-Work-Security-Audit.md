# Security Briefing — Remote Work Device Security Audit
**Course:** CMIT 425 — Cybersecurity Management | UMGC  
**Author:** Brandy Haynes  

---

## Overview

This briefing outlines the planning and execution of a scheduled security audit for company devices used in the remote Work-From-Home program. The audit analyzes how employees use laptops and devices, noting how corporate and non-corporate systems are being accessed and identifying any misuse or security issues.

---

## Audit Goals

- Assign appropriate roles and responsibilities to personnel based on experience, position, and qualifications
- Define the scope of the audit — specifying which aspects of WFH devices need examination and what policies need to be established
- Compile a list of data and assets to protect, including hardware, software, information databases, and internal or legal documentation

---

## Areas Under Investigation

- Establishing a robust remote and work-from-home security strategy
- Ensuring network security
- Managing authentication and authorization processes
- Securing collaborative software and applications
- Ensuring new policies reflect the current security needs of the company

---

## Key Security Recommendations

### Security Policy Foundation
Team members benefit from clear, concise guidelines that are regularly reviewed and updated to meet evolving technical needs. Standards, expectations, procedures, and processes must be clearly defined for staff adherence.

### BYOD (Bring Your Own Device) Risk Management
- Employees may have stored company documents on personal devices or sent drafts to personal email — significantly increasing the risk profile
- All team members should consistently employ encryption on both company and personal devices used for work
- Robust authentication methods including **multi-factor authentication (MFA)** should be used alongside strong passwords

### Identity and Access Management
- Remote workers require automated provisioning solutions for appropriate access to data and network resources
- A strong identity and access management (IAM) solution automates and adjusts provisioning measures as needed

### Network Security
- Employees should be discouraged from using unsecured public networks or hotspots to access company resources
- **VPN (Virtual Private Network)** technology should be the standard method for accessing any business resources remotely

### Third-Party Application Security
- Third-party collaboration tools (chat platforms, shared workspaces) must be secured and monitored
- Managing access to these platforms ensures proper utilization and prevents misuse

---

## Audit Scope — Additional Topics

- Potential cyber-related attacks and threat response
- Encouraging use of cameras during remote meetings for identity verification
- Preparing for upcoming changes in systems and policies

---

## Conclusion

The increase in remote work presents new challenges for companies and their legal and moral obligations to protect employee and organizational data. A transparent, efficient, and practical auditing process benefits everyone — building trust while identifying and resolving security gaps before they become breaches.
