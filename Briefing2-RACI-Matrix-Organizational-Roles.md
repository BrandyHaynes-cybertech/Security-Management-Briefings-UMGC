## Overview

Ensuring the integrity and confidentiality of data and resources requires all employees to work together. This briefing outlines how to use a RACI matrix to define distinct roles and responsibilities for protecting the integrity and confidentiality of network data and resources.

---

## RACI Key

| Key | Role | Definition |
|-----|------|------------|
| **R** | Responsible | Performs the task |
| **A** | Accountable | Has ultimate accountability for correct and thorough completion |
| **C** | Consulted | Provides input before decisions or actions are taken |
| **I** | Informed | Notified after decisions and actions are taken |

## Role Descriptions

### Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- Responsible for everyday tasks: generating information, planning, operations, finances & budget
- First held accountable for management and data loss prevention technologies
- Typically holds the highest rank on the board of directors
- Kept in the loop on all current events and decisions being made

### Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Responsible for managing company finances
- Determines organizational needs and how to budget for them
- Oversees financial and accounting services
- Must be kept informed of anything affecting the financial side of the business

### Chief Information Officer (CIO)
- Often managed by the CEO or CFO depending on organizational needs
- Responsible for management and usage of technology and information systems
- Works between technology systems and the business side of the organization
- Must be informed on every level of the business

### Chief Privacy Officer (CPO)
- Keeps company, employee, and customer information secure
- Ensures data does not fall into the wrong hands
- Responsible for controlling data and privacy
- Develops and enforces privacy and security standards and policies

### Chief Security Officer (CSO)
- Recognizes and reduces potential risks
- Understands company goals to develop a tailored security program
- Ensures constant availability of company systems
- Vigilantly mitigates security risks

### Managers
- Direct connection between upper management and employees
- Delegates duties and carries out instructions from upper management
- Controls how objectives will be completed
- Active in day-to-day operations

### Supervisors
- Ensures managers' objectives are carried out to specific standards
- Oversees specific operations
- Confirms employees are following set guidelines and regulations

### Employees
- Have the least number of privileges
- Take direction from management
- Ensure standards, guidelines, and policies are met
- Utilize information generated by upper management based on their own duties

---

## Key Takeaway

Not everyone needs access to all information within the company. Some employees only need awareness of certain information to complete tasks, while others must understand entire areas of the business to manage their own roles. Executive positions hold the highest power, the highest need-to-know, and the greatest responsibilities.
