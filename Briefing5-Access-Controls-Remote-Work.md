# Security Briefing — Access Controls & Remote Work Security
**Course:** CMIT 425 — Cybersecurity Management | UMGC  
**Author:** Brandy Haynes  
**Date:** June 28, 2024  

---

## Overview

Access controls are a key security feature for protecting corporate assets. Their chief purpose is to safeguard assets from any form of unauthorized access. Think of access control models as frameworks that enforce the rules and objectives of an organization's security model — dictating how subjects can access objects or files.

---

## Why Access Controls Matter

- Required to enhance security compliance programs and regulatory requirements
- Control and regulate who or what can view, edit, or access corporate assets and information processing systems
- Reduce the risk of data breaches and unauthorized intrusion
- Minimize unauthorized access in both physical and logical instances
- Limit access to networks, files, applications, computer systems, and sensitive data such as PII (Personally Identifiable Information)

---

## Access Controls in Work-From-Home Environments

Access controls are especially critical in remote work environments where the traditional network perimeter no longer exists. Key considerations include:

- Ensuring employees can only access resources needed for their specific role
- Monitoring and logging all access attempts to corporate systems
- Implementing multi-factor authentication (MFA) for remote access
- Regularly reviewing and updating access permissions as roles change

---

## Common Roadblocks Employees Face

When access controls are improperly configured, employees may experience:

- Frustration due to the highly distributed nature of modern IT systems
- Time required to learn and navigate dynamic access control designs
- Fatigue from managing data governance, visibility, and constant reporting requirements
- Password fatigue — especially when multiple passwords are required for different systems
- Frequent authorization and authentication issues requiring supervisor intervention
- Reduced productivity when access controls create unnecessary bottlenecks

---

## Why Disabling Access Controls is Never Acceptable

Turning off access controls poses severe risks:

- Significantly increases vulnerability to exploitation by bad actors
- Increases the attack surface for diverse types of cyberattacks
- May allow unauthorized users onto employee home networks — intrusions that are difficult to detect even for trained professionals
- Creates compliance violations that could result in legal and regulatory consequences

---

## Insider Threats in Remote Work Environments

Remote work increases exposure to insider threats including:

- **Malicious insiders** — disgruntled employees with access to company resources who may sell data on the dark web
- **Non-malicious insiders** — employees targeted by external actors to unknowingly expose company data
- **External attackers** targeting remote workers specifically to breach critical data or commit fraud
- **Dark web recruitment** — malicious actors hiring current employees to infiltrate the company network from the inside

---

## Conclusion

Access controls are essential security features that regulate how users interact with an organization's network resources. They are non-negotiable in work-from-home environments — disabling them dramatically increases vulnerability to unauthorized access, data breaches, fraud, and insider threats. Proper implementation, configuration, and regular review of access controls is a foundational requirement for any secure remote work program.
